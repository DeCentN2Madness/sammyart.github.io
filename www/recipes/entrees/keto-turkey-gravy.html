Keto Turkey Gravy

This is a flavorful addition to any roast turkey or chicken dinner. Try it on wild rice with sautéed mushrooms for a seriously decadent treat!

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup butter
  • 2 cups turkey drippings
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1/4 tsp salt (or to taste)
  • 1 tbsp xanthan gum

Instructions

  1. In a medium saucepan over medium heat, melt the butter. Add the chicken broth, turkey drippings, and salt to the pan, then bring to a boil.
  2. Add the xanthan gum into the pan while using an immersion blender until the desired consistency is reached.

Notes

You can use turkey drippings from a whole turkey or bone-in turkey breast. If you have neither, use chicken bone broth instead! Regular chicken broth doesn't replace the drippings very well and is not recommended as the replacement for that ingredient.

Use the gibblets from a turkey to add some great texture to the gravy. Simmer them while the turkey bakes, adding water as needed to keep covered. Cut the gibblets into small pieces and drop them into the saucepan just before adding the xanthan gum.

You can use gelatin in place of the xathan gum. Simply sprinkle 3 tbsp unflavored gelatin over 1 cup cold water and set it aside to bloom while getting the rest of the gravy ready in the saucepan. Once the butter, broth, and drippings come to a boil, simmer for about 20 minutes to let the volume drop by about half. Slowly add the gelatin while immersion blending or whisking vigorously until dissolved.

Servings: 12
Serving Size: 2 tbsp (very rich)
Prep Time: 5 mins
Cook Time: 20 mins
Total Time: 25 mins
