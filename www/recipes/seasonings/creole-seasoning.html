Creole Seasoning (No Black Pepper)

A simple, Tony Chachere’s–style Creole blend that skips black pepper but keeps the bold, salty, garlicky heat. Great on shrimp, chicken, catfish, fries, eggs, gumbo, and jambalaya.

Ingredients

  • 3 Tbsp kosher salt (or 2 Tbsp fine table salt)
  • 2 Tbsp paprika
  • 1 Tbsp garlic powder
  • 1 Tbsp onion powder
  • 1 Tbsp mild American-style chili powder
  • 1 tsp cayenne pepper (to taste)
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1/2 tsp crushed red pepper flakes
  • Optional: 1/2 tsp white pepper (for a gentle pepper note without black specks)

Instructions

  1. Add all ingredients to a bowl and whisk until completely uniform.
  2. Transfer to a clean, airtight jar. Label and date.
  3. For best flavor, let the blend rest 24 hours before using.
  4. Use in place of salt: start with about 1 tsp per lb (450 g) of protein or a light sprinkle over veggies, fries, eggs, and rice; adjust to taste.

Notes

Salt-forward by design—season food with this instead of adding extra salt. For a lighter sodium option, reduce kosher salt to 2 Tbsp and add 1 Tbsp extra paprika to maintain volume. Prefer smokiness? Swap half the paprika for smoked paprika. Spicier? Increase cayenne to 1½–2 tsp or add more red pepper flakes. 

Yield: ~1/2 cup (about 8 Tbsp)
Prep Time: 5 mins
Cook Time: 0 mins
Total Time: 5 mins
Storage: Airtight, in a cool dark place, up to 6 months
Sodium Note: Use 2 Tbsp if substituting fine table salt
Heat Level: Medium (adjust cayenne to taste)
