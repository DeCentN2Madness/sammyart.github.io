Gluten-Free Carrot Cake (No Sugar, No Nuts, Yolks-Only)

This carrot cake is for the days you want the classic bakery experience… without gluten, refined sugar, nuts, or egg whites.

Instead of wheat flour and sugar, this recipe uses:

  • Oat flour, brown rice flour, and tapioca starch for a tender crumb
  • Egg yolks only for richness and structure
  • Lakanto monk fruit/erythritol blend for sweetness
  • A touch of heavy cream for a lush texture with relatively low lactose
  • Plenty of finely grated carrot to keep the cake moist

The result is a moist, rich, but not overly dense carrot cake that you can stack into a beautiful two-layer 9-inch cake with cream cheese frosting.

Why These Ingredients Work (and How They Play Together)

The flour blend: oat, brown rice, and tapioca

Instead of a premade gluten-free mix, this cake uses a simple trio:

  • Oat flour (certified gluten-free)

    • Brings a gentle, almost “homey” flavor and tenderness.
    • Helps prevent the cake from feeling gritty.

  • Brown rice flour

    • Adds structure so the layers slice cleanly.
    • Balances the softness of oat flour so the cake doesn’t collapse.

  • Tapioca starch

    • Adds lightness and a bit of chew.
    • Helps the cake stay moist rather than crumbly.

Together, they mimic the behavior of cake flour: tender, not gummy, and strong enough to hold up as a layered cake.

Egg yolks only: rich and gentle

We skip egg whites entirely and use yolks only, which:

  • Emulsify fat and liquids, helping the batter stay smooth
  • Add richness and color
  • Contribute to structure without the rubbery bounce that whites can sometimes create in gluten-free baking

To back this up, we add an optional flax “egg” that behaves like a bit of gentle scaffolding for the crumb.

Sweetness without sugar: Lakanto + optional honey or banana

For the main sweetness, this recipe uses:

  • Lakanto Classic monk fruit/erythritol blend (1:1 sugar replacement)

    • Bakes like sugar in volume.
    • Does not caramelize the same way sugar does, so the cake won’t brown quite as deeply.
    • Erythritol gives a slight “cooling” sensation, which can be softened with…

  • Optional honey

    • Adds floral notes and just a little real sugar for those who tolerate it.
    • Helps with moisture and a more classic mouthfeel.

  • Optional very ripe banana

    • Adds natural sweetness and additional moisture.
    • Slightly denser crumb, but lovely if you like a hint of banana.

If you must strictly avoid all sugar, simply omit the honey and use only Lakanto (and banana if desired).

Cream, yogurt, and oil: balancing richness and moisture

Instead of regular milk, we use a heavy cream + water combo, plus yogurt and oil:

  • Heavy cream + water

    • Cream adds fat and tenderness while staying relatively low in lactose.
    • Thinning it with water keeps the batter from becoming too heavy.

  • Full-fat yogurt (dairy or coconut)

    • Adds tang and moisture.
    • Reacts with baking soda for a better rise.

  • Neutral oil (like avocado, sunflower, or light olive)

    • Stays fluid when chilled, so the cake doesn’t turn hard in the fridge.
    • Helps the crumb feel soft and moist for days.

Carrots and flavor boosters

  • Very finely grated carrot

    • Disappears into the crumb, releasing moisture as it bakes.
    • Keeps the cake naturally sweet and tender.

  • Warm spices: cinnamon, ginger, nutmeg

    • Make the cake taste like carrot cake even without sugar.

  • Optional shredded coconut or orange zest

    • Coconut adds richness and texture (no nuts involved).
    • Orange zest brightens the flavors and works beautifully with carrot and cream cheese.

Recipe: Double-Layer 9-Inch Gluten-Free Carrot Cake (No Sugar, No Nuts, Yolks Only)

Yield: Two 9-inch round layers (10–12 servings) Sweetener: Formulated for Lakanto Classic monk fruit/erythritol Oven: 350°F / 175°C

Dry ingredients

  • 2 cups (200 g) oat flour (certified gluten-free)
  • 1½ cups (180 g) brown rice flour
  • ½ cup (60 g) tapioca starch
  • 3 tsp baking powder (gluten-free)
  • ¾ tsp baking soda
  • ¾ tsp fine sea salt
  • 4 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tsp ground ginger
  • ½ tsp ground nutmeg

Egg & binder

  • 8 large egg yolks, room temperature (no whites)
  • 2 Tbsp ground flaxseed (optional but recommended)
  • 6 Tbsp warm water (for the flax)

Sweeteners & wet ingredients

  • 1⅓ cups (about 260 g) Lakanto Classic monk fruit/erythritol blend

  • 3–4 Tbsp runny honey, optional

    • Use the higher end if you like a sweeter cake and tolerate a little sugar.
  • ½ cup (about 120 g) very ripe banana, well mashed (optional but nice)
  • ⅔ cup (160 ml) neutral oil (avocado, sunflower, or light olive)
  • ½ cup (120 g) full-fat plain yogurt (dairy or coconut)

  • Cream mixture:

    • ½ cup (120 ml) heavy cream
    • ¼ cup (60 ml) water (or unsweetened plant milk)
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 tsp lemon juice or apple cider vinegar

Carrot & optional extras

  • 3 cups (about 360 g) very finely grated carrot, lightly packed
  • (Optional) ¼ cup unsweetened shredded coconut
  • (Optional) 1–2 tsp orange zest

Sugar-free cream cheese frosting (Lakanto)

  • 16 oz (450 g) cream cheese, softened
  • 8 Tbsp (112 g) soft butter (or soft coconut oil for dairy-reduced, though flavor will change)
  • ⅔–1 cup powdered Lakanto (blend granular Lakanto in a blender to powder it)
  • 2–3 Tbsp honey, optional, for smoother texture and flavor
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1–3 tsp milk, cream, or water as needed to adjust consistency

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Prepare pans and oven

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F / 175°C.
  2. Grease two 9-inch round cake pans and line the bottoms with parchment circles. Lightly grease the parchment as well.

2. Make the flax binder (optional)

  1. In a small bowl, stir together 2 Tbsp ground flaxseed and 6 Tbsp warm water.
  2. Let it stand for about 5–10 minutes until thick and gel-like.

This adds subtle structure, which is especially helpful in gluten-free, yolks-only baking.

3. Whisk the dry ingredients

In a medium bowl, whisk together:

  • Oat flour, brown rice flour, tapioca starch
  • Baking powder, baking soda, salt
  • Cinnamon, ginger, nutmeg

Set aside.

4. Build the yolk + sweetener base

  1. In a large mixing bowl, add 8 egg yolks and 1⅓ cups Lakanto.

  2. Using a hand mixer or vigorous whisking, beat for 2–3 minutes, until the mixture is a bit lighter and slightly thickened.

    • This step incorporates air and helps compensate for the lack of egg whites.

  3. Beat in:

    • Honey (if using)
    • Mashed banana (if using)
    • Oil
    • Yogurt
    • The cream + water mixture
    • Vanilla extract
    • Lemon juice or vinegar

  4. Stir in the flax gel if you’re using it.

5. Add carrots and flavor boosters

  1. Fold the finely grated carrot into the wet mixture until evenly distributed.
  2. Stir in the shredded coconut and/or orange zest if you’re using them.

6. Combine wet and dry

  1. Sprinkle the dry ingredients over the wet in two additions, gently folding with a spatula.

  2. Fold just until you no longer see dry flour.

    • The batter will be thick but scoopable.
    • If it seems extremely stiff, you can add 1–2 tsp of water to loosen it slightly, but avoid making it runny.

7. Bake the layers

  1. Divide the batter evenly between the two prepared 9-inch pans and smooth the tops.

  2. Bake at 350°F / 175°C for 25–32 minutes.

    • The edges will be just pulling from the sides.
    • A toothpick or skewer inserted in the center should come out with a few moist crumbs but no wet batter.

  3. Cool the cakes in the pans on a rack for 15 minutes.

  4. Run a knife around the edges, invert each layer onto the rack, peel off parchment, and let them cool completely before frosting.

8. Make the Lakanto cream cheese frosting

  1. In a large bowl, beat the softened cream cheese and butter until very smooth and fluffy.
  2. Add ⅔ cup powdered Lakanto, vanilla, salt, and 2 Tbsp honey (if using). Beat well.

  3. Taste and adjust:

    • Add more powdered Lakanto if you want it sweeter.
    • Add another 1 Tbsp honey if you tolerate sugar and want a rounder flavor.
    • If frosting is too thick, beat in 1–3 tsp milk, cream, or water.
    • If it is too soft, chill for 10–15 minutes.

9. Frost and assemble

  1. Place one cooled cake layer on your serving plate.
  2. Spread a generous layer of frosting over the top.
  3. Place the second layer on top, upside-down for a flatter surface, and frost the top and sides.

  4. Optionally garnish with:

    • Extra grated carrot (very lightly)
    • A sprinkle of shredded coconut
    • A little orange zest

Refrigerate to set the frosting, then slice and enjoy. The cake keeps well covered in the fridge for several days.

Pan Size Guide (What to Expect)

This recipe is written for two 9-inch round pans, but you have options:

1. Single 9×13-inch pan

  • Use the full recipe for a thicker snack cake.
  • Bake at 350°F / 175°C for about 30–38 minutes.
  • The center takes a bit longer to set; use the toothpick test.

2. Two 8-inch round pans

  • Use the full recipe.
  • Layers will be slightly thicker than 9-inch rounds.
  • Bake about 28–35 minutes.
  • Cool thoroughly; thicker layers are more fragile when warm.

3. Cupcakes

  • This recipe will make roughly 24 standard cupcakes.
  • Fill liners about ⅔ full.
  • Bake at 350°F / 175°C for 16–20 minutes.
  • Cupcakes will dome slightly and may crack a little more than wheat-based ones (normal with erythritol).
  • Cool completely before frosting so the liners don’t peel away.

4. Thinner sheet cake (12×17 jelly roll)

  • For a thinner layer (great for cutting into squares), you can:

    • Use about ¾ of the batter, or
    • Use all of it and accept a slightly thicker layer.
  • Bake 15–22 minutes, checking early.

Sweetener Swaps: What Changes?

This cake is calibrated for Lakanto Classic (monk fruit + erythritol, 1:1 sugar replacement). If you’d like to try something else:

Using another monk fruit/erythritol brand (1:1 sugar)

  • If the label says it measures like sugar, you can usually swap cup-for-cup.
  • Expect minor differences in sweetness and browning.
  • Some blends taste slightly sweeter; if unsure, start with about 1¼ cups instead of 1⅓ cups, then taste the batter (safe because no raw whites) and add more if needed.

Using pure erythritol or a different sugar alcohol blend

  • Many pure erythritol products are slightly less sweet than sugar by volume.
  • You may want to add a bit more (e.g., 1½ cups) or include honey/banana to round out the sweetness and texture.

Using stevia or pure monk fruit drops

  • These are far more concentrated and don’t provide bulk.

  • You will need:

    • A separate bulk ingredient (like extra yogurt or a small amount of additional flour/liquid balance).
    • Careful adjustment to avoid bitterness.
  • This becomes a different, more experimental recipe; if you’re comfortable tinkering, start by following the base recipe but replacing part of the Lakanto with stevia/monk fruit, not all of it at once.

Substitutions & Variations

Dairy-light or dairy-free

  • Heavy cream & yogurt:

    • You can replace both with full-fat canned coconut milk (well stirred) and coconut yogurt.
    • Keep the total liquid amount roughly the same and watch the batter consistency—it should be thick but scoopable.

  • Frosting:

    • Use a dairy-free cream cheese and plant butter, sweetened with powdered Lakanto.
    • Texture will differ slightly, but it works.

Less lactose (but not dairy-free)

  • Use lactose-free yogurt and lactose-free cream cheese, if available.
  • Heavy cream is naturally low in lactose, and we’re using a modest amount.

Without banana or honey

  • Omit banana and/or honey and simply keep:

    • The Lakanto as written, and
    • The yogurt + cream combo as written.
  • The cake will be slightly less sweet and very slightly less moist, but still rich and tender—do not overbake.

Texture tweaks

  • Lighter, more delicate crumb:

    • Reduce brown rice flour to 1¼ cups (150 g) and increase tapioca to ¾ cup (90 g).
    • This gives a bit more lift and softness but can be slightly more fragile to stack.

  • More spice-forward:

    • Add an extra ½ tsp cinnamon and ¼ tsp cloves for a deeper spice profile.

Gluten-Free Carrot Cake (Monk Fruit Sweetened, Double 9" Layers)

Gluten-free No egg whites No refined sugar Nut-free Yield: 2 × 9" round layers (about 12 servings)

Ingredients

Dry ingredients

  • 2 cups (200 g) oat flour (certified gluten-free)
  • 1½ cups (180 g) brown rice flour
  • ½ cup (60 g) tapioca starch
  • 3 tsp baking powder (gluten-free)
  • ¾ tsp baking soda
  • ¾ tsp fine sea salt
  • 4 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tsp ground ginger
  • ½ tsp ground nutmeg

Egg and binder

  • 8 large egg yolks, room temperature (no whites)
  • 2 Tbsp ground flaxseed (optional but helpful for structure)
  • 6 Tbsp warm water (for the flaxseed)

Sweeteners and wet ingredients

  • 1⅓ cups (about 260 g) Lakanto Classic monk fruit/erythritol
  • 3–4 Tbsp runny honey (optional)
  • ½ cup (about 120 g) very ripe banana, well-mashed (optional)
  • ⅔ cup (160 ml) neutral oil (such as avocado or light olive)
  • ½ cup (120 g) full-fat plain yogurt (dairy or coconut)
  • ½ cup (120 ml) heavy cream
  • ¼ cup (60 ml) water (or unsweetened plant milk)
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 tsp lemon juice or apple cider vinegar

Carrot and optional extras

  • 3 cups (about 360 g) very finely grated carrot, lightly packed
  • ¼ cup unsweetened shredded coconut (optional)
  • 1–2 tsp orange zest (optional)

Sugar-free cream cheese frosting

  • 16 oz (450 g) cream cheese, softened
  • 8 Tbsp (112 g) soft butter (or soft coconut oil)
  • ⅔–1 cup powdered Lakanto (blend the granular sweetener until fine)
  • 2–3 Tbsp honey (optional, for flavor and smoothness)
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • Pinch of fine sea salt
  • 1–3 tsp milk, cream, or water, as needed to loosen

Instructions

1. Prepare the pans and oven

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Grease two 9-inch round cake pans. Line the bottoms with parchment circles and lightly grease the parchment.

2. Mix the optional flax binder

  1. In a small bowl, stir together the ground flaxseed and warm water.
  2. Let sit for 5–10 minutes until thick and gel-like.

3. Whisk the dry ingredients

  1. In a medium bowl, whisk together the oat flour, brown rice flour, tapioca starch, baking powder, baking soda, salt, cinnamon, ginger, and nutmeg.
  2. Set aside.

4. Build the yolk and sweetener base

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the egg yolks and Lakanto. Beat with a hand mixer or whisk vigorously for 2–3 minutes, until slightly lighter and thickened.
  2. Beat in the honey (if using), mashed banana (if using), oil, yogurt, heavy cream, water, vanilla, and lemon juice or vinegar until smooth.
  3. Add the thickened flax mixture and stir to combine.

5. Add the carrots and extras

  1. Fold the grated carrot into the wet mixture until evenly spread.
  2. Stir in shredded coconut and orange zest, if using, until just combined.

6. Combine wet and dry

  1. Sprinkle the dry mixture over the wet ingredients in two additions, folding gently with a spatula after each addition.
  2. Fold just until no dry streaks remain. The batter should be thick but scoopable. If it is extremely stiff, add 1–2 tsp water to loosen very slightly.

7. Bake the cake layers

  1. Divide the batter evenly between the two prepared pans and smooth the tops.
  2. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 25–32 minutes. The edges should be just pulling from the sides, and a toothpick in the center should come out with a few moist crumbs but no wet batter.

8. Cool completely

  1. Cool the cakes in the pans on a rack for 15 minutes.
  2. Run a knife around the edges, invert onto a rack, peel off the parchment, and cool completely before frosting.

9. Make the cream cheese frosting

  1. In a large bowl, beat the softened cream cheese and butter until very smooth and fluffy.
  2. Add ⅔ cup powdered Lakanto, vanilla, salt, and 2 Tbsp honey (if using). Beat until smooth. Adjust sweetness with more powdered Lakanto and honey if desired.
  3. If the frosting is too thick, beat in 1–3 tsp milk, cream, or water. If it is too soft, chill for 10–15 minutes to firm up.

10. Assemble and frost

  1. Place one cooled cake layer on a serving plate.
  2. Spread a generous layer of frosting over the top.
  3. Place the second layer on top (bottom side up for a flatter surface) and frost the top and sides.
  4. Chill briefly to set the frosting, then slice into about 12 servings.
Other pan sizes: For a 9×13-inch pan, use the full batter and bake 30–38 minutes. For cupcakes, fill liners about two thirds full and bake 16–20 minutes.
