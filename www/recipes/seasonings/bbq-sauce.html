Kick-to-the-Ribs OMGWTFBBQ Suace

This recipe provides tons of flavor along with a few control knobs that may help facilitate your journey into subjective perfection.

Ingredients

Base

  • 1/2 cup balsamic vinegar
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1/4 cup stout beer
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1 (6 oz) can tomato paste
  • 2 Tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 tsp yellow mustard
  • 2 Tbsp coconut aminos
  • 3/4 tsp salt

Fresh aromatics

  • 1/2 cup onion, finely diced (about 1/2 a small onion; yellow is great)
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced

Heat + smoke

  • 1 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp chipotle powder (use 1 tsp for hotter/smokier)
  • 1/4 tsp cayenne to start (up to 1/2 tsp for hot)
  • 1/4 tsp crushed red pepper flakes

Sweetener

  • 2 tsp Lakanto Classic 1:1

For sautéing

  • 1 tsp avocado oil or olive oil

Instructions

  1. Sauté aromatics: In a small saucepan over medium heat, add the oil, then the diced onion. Cook 4–6 minutes until softened and starting to turn translucent. Add garlic and cook 30–60 seconds until fragrant (don’t brown it).
  2. Build the sauce: Add tomato paste and stir for 30 seconds to “toast” it a bit. Then whisk in balsamic vinegar, apple cider vinegar, stout, water, Dijon and yellow mustards, coconut aminos, salt, smoked paprika, chipotle powder, cayenne, and crushed red pepper flakes.
  3. Simmer to mellow stout + blend flavors: Bring to a gentle simmer, then lower heat and simmer 15–20 minutes, stirring often.
  4. Blend smooth: Use an immersion blender right in the pot, or blend carefully in a regular blender until completely smooth.
  5. Thicken: Toward the end of blending, add a tiny pinch (~1/32 tsp) of Xanthan gum while blending. Wait 60 seconds and repeat if needed. Usually 1/16 tsp total is plenty.
  6. Texturize (optional): an additional 1/4 tsp Crushed Red Pepper Flakes may be added at this point for noticeable-but-not-dominant texture, 1/2 tsp for a more “speckled” dip.
  7. Reduce: Gently simmer 3–5 minutes more until dip-thick and pepper flakes softened (if using).
  8. Sweeten last: Stir in 2 tsp Lakanto, taste, then add another tsp only if the sauce needs rounding.

Notes

Allow to cool for 15 minutes before serving. This will both thicken the sauce and balance the heat.

To adjust heat, twiddle the cayenne knob.

To adjust smoky bite, twiddle the chipotle knob.

If too tangy/roasty, add another tsp monk fruit or a pinch of salt.

If too sweet, add 1-2 tsp apple cider vinegar and a pinch of salt.

Local raw wild honey may be substituted in place of the monk fruit blend, but use half the volume (start with 1 tsp).
